# PPC campaign brief

Read the approved `content/site-brief.md` first. Ask only for campaign facts that are missing or unresolved there:

1. Offer: what is being promoted, the price or commitment, and any real incentive.
2. Audience and intent: who clicked, the campaign/channel, and the query or promise that brought them here.
3. Conversion: one primary action such as purchase, booking, call, application, or lead form.
4. Evidence: approved testimonials, numbers, reviews, certifications, guarantees, screenshots, or case studies.
5. Objections: the top reasons a qualified visitor may hesitate.
6. Brand: name, logo, palette, tone, and supplied media.
7. Destination: replace the homepage or create a dedicated campaign route.
8. Compliance constraints: regulated claims, required disclosures, geographic restrictions, and offer dates.

Default only low-risk structure decisions. Never default business facts, proof, legal claims, or pricing.

## Recommended narrative

1. Hero: message-matched promise, audience qualifier, CTA, and offer context.
2. Outcomes: three concrete benefits tied to the campaign intent.
3. Proof: only supplied evidence; otherwise clearly marked proof placeholders.
4. Mechanism: explain how the offer produces the outcome.
5. Objections: address friction, eligibility, timing, or risk.
6. Final CTA: repeat the same conversion action and destination.

Use fewer sections when the offer is simple. A landing page is not a compressed marketing website.
